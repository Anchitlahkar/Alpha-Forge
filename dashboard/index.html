Controlling Reasoning Effort in LLMs

AI Research Signal: 7.0
📅 Sat, 18 Jul 2026 11:16:09 GMT 🌐 Source: Ahead of AI
💡 Why It Matters
This article is crucial for anyone working with or investing in LLMs, as it demystifies how modern models like GPT-5.6 offer varying 'reasoning effort' levels. Understanding these mechanisms allows for more efficient and effective use of LLMs, balancing computational cost, latency, and answer quality. It highlights the evolution from always-on verbose reasoning to controllable, nuanced reasoning modes, which is vital for practical application development and strategic decision-making in the AI space.
📝 Summary (TLDR)
The article explains how LLMs learn and offer different 'reasoning effort' modes, from basic on/off switches to multi-level settings. It covers training methods like RLVR, the role of cosmetic `` tokens, and how system prompts or tokenizer settings enable these controls, directly impacting response length, quality, and computational cost.
📌 Key Points
  • Reasoning models output intermediate step-by-step 'reasoning traces' to solve problems, differing from conventional LLMs that provide direct answers.
  • DeepSeek-R1 popularized Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) for training reasoning models, using verifiable data (math, code) for reward signals (0/1 correctness).
  • Models can learn to self-correct ('Aha' moments) even when only the final answer's correctness is rewarded during training, not the intermediate reasoning trace itself.
  • Inference scaling (e.g., self-consistency, self-refinement) improves performance by spending more compute during usage, often in addition to reasoning training.
  • Cosmetic `` tokens are used to delimit reasoning traces for UI purposes and are encouraged via formatting rewards, but are not essential for the model's reasoning ability.
  • Modern LLMs (e.g., Qwen3, GPT-5.6) offer 'reasoning mode' on/off switches or multiple 'reasoning effort' settings, allowing users to control verbosity, quality, and compute.
  • These effort settings are often implemented by prepending specific instructions (e.g., 'Reasoning effort: low/medium/high') to the system prompt or via tokenizer settings, directly correlating with response length and accuracy.
⚡ Action Items
  • Experiment with different reasoning effort settings in LLM APIs (e.g., GPT-5.6) to understand their impact on response quality, latency, and token usage for specific tasks.
  • For developers, consider how to integrate reasoning effort controls into applications to optimize user experience and operational costs.
  • Stay informed about ongoing research into incorporating intermediate reasoning traces directly into LLM training signals for potentially more robust reasoning capabilities.
  • If training custom LLMs, explore RLVR techniques and the 'Thinking Mode Fusion' approach to enable controllable reasoning behaviors.
