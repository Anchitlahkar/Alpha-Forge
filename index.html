📌 Key Points
  • Project Galileo has provided free cybersecurity services to over 3,400 websites of journalists, human rights defenders, and nonprofits in 120 countries for 12 years.
  • Cloudflare released its first comprehensive annual report on cyberattacks targeting global civil society, leveraging unique data from Project Galileo's participants.
  • Key findings from the report indicate civil society organizations are targeted more frequently and intensely than other internet users, with DDoS attacks being common and prolonged, exploit attempts 7x higher, and journalists in exile facing 4x higher malicious traffic.
  • The report calls for ensuring simple and affordable cybersecurity for all, expanding transparency about cyberattacks and internet shutdowns, and embedding AI and post-quantum protections into security tools by default.
  • Cloudflare published 16 qualitative case studies detailing the security needs of Project Galileo participants.
  • Three new partners (International Center for Journalists, Media Cluster Norway, NGO-ISAC) were announced, focusing on supporting journalists and civil society organizations.
  • Project Galileo recently added services to help local news organizations protect their content from AI crawlers.
